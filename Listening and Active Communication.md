# Listening and Active Communication

## Question 1: What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?

1. Give full attention to the speaker.
2. Maintain eye contact and positive body language.
3. Avoid interrupting while others are speaking.
4. Ask questions when something is unclear.
5. Listen to understand, not just to respond.
6. Summarize or repeat key points to confirm understanding.

---

## Question 2: According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?

Reflective listening means carefully listening to the speaker and understanding both their message and emotions. Then, we restate the message in our own words to confirm that we understood it correctly.

---

## Question 3: What are the obstacles in your listening process?

I sometimes get distracted by my phone or other thoughts while listening. I also tend to think about my response before the other person finishes speaking.

---

## Question 4: What can you do to improve your listening?

I can improve my listening by staying focused, avoiding distractions, and taking notes during important conversations. I should also ask questions and summarize key points to ensure understanding.

---

## Question 5: When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

I become passive when talking to seniors or people in authority. Sometimes I avoid sharing my opinions to prevent arguments or conflicts.

---

## Question 6: When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

I may become aggressive when I am stressed, frustrated, or when work is delayed despite repeated reminders. This usually happens during high-pressure situations.

---

## Question 7: When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

I sometimes become passive-aggressive when I am upset but do not express my feelings directly. Instead, I may give short replies or use sarcasm to show my frustration.

---

## Question 8: How can you make your communication assertive?

I can make my communication assertive by expressing my thoughts clearly and respectfully. I should listen carefully, stay calm, use "I" statements, and focus on solving problems rather than blaming others.
